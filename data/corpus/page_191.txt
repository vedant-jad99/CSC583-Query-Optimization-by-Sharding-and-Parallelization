In June 2018, it was announced that a speech of Hawking's,
set to music by Greek composer Vangelis, would be beamed
into space from a European Space Agency satellite dish in
Spain in the direction of the nearest black hole, 1A 0620-
00.[374]
On 8 November 2018, an auction of 22 personal possessions of Hawking, including his doctoral thesis
(Properties of Expanding Universes, PhD thesis, Cambridge University, 1965) and wheelchair, took
place, and fetched about £1.8 million.[375][376] Proceeds from the auction sale of the wheelchair went to
two charities, the Motor Neurone Disease Association and the Stephen Hawking Foundation;[377]
proceeds from the other items went to his estate.[376]
In March 2019, it was announced that the Royal Mint would issue a commemorative 50p coin, only
available as a commemorative edition,[378] in honour of Hawking.[379] The same month, Hawking's
nurse, Patricia Dowdy, was struck off the nursing register for "failures over his care and financial
misconduct".[380]
In May 2021, it was announced that an Acceptance-in-Lieu agreement between HMRC, the Department
for Culture, Media and Sport, Cambridge University Library, Science Museum Group, and the Hawking
Estate, would see around 10,000 pages of Hawking's scientific and other papers remain in Cambridge,
while objects including his wheelchairs, speech synthesisers, and personal memorabilia from his former
Cambridge office would be housed at the Science Museum.[381] In February 2022, the "Stephen Hawking
at Work" display opened at the Science Museum, London as the start of a two-year nationwide tour.[382]
In October 2024, the Hawking Building was opened to the public at the Science and Innovation Park as a
new facility to house the Science Museum's National Collection Centre which includes about 80% of the
museum's object collection.[383]
