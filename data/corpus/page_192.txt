Hawking appeared in multiple documentary films, including God, the Universe and Everything Else
(1988),[384] The Real Stephen Hawking (2001),[332] Stephen Hawking: Profile (2002),[385] and
Hawking (2013),[386] and the documentary series Stephen Hawking, Master of the Universe
(2008).[387]
Appearing on the radio programme Desert Island Discs in 1992, Hawking chose multiple musical works
that "[sum] up my life": Mozart's Requiem; The Beatles' "Please Please Me"; Édith Piaf's "Non, je ne
regrette rien"; Francis Poulenc's Gloria; Johannes Brahms' Violin Concerto; Ludwig van Beethoven's
String Quartet No. 15; Richard Wagner's Die Walküre, Act 1; and "O Principe, che a lunghe carovane", a
segment of Giacomo Puccini's opera Turandot. For a book, he chose George Eliot's Middlemarch.[33]
In 1993, Hawking's synthesiser voice was recorded for use in the Pink Floyd song "Keep
Talking",[388][172] and in 1999, it was recorded for an episode of The Simpsons.[389] Hawking
copyrighted the voice, and allowed it to be used in The Theory of Everything, in which he was portrayed
by Eddie Redmayne in an Academy Award-winning role.[390][391][392]
Hawking played a holographic simulation of himself in an
episode of Star Trek: The Next Generation in 1993.[393][394]
Hawking also guest-starred in the sitcoms Futurama and The
Big Bang Theory.[178][395] Broadcast in March 2018, before
his death, Hawking was the voice of The Book Mark II on The
Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy radio series, and he was a
guest of Neil deGrasse Tyson on StarTalk.[396] Hawking was
featured at the Monty Python Live (Mostly) show in 2014,
shown in a pre-recorded video singing Monty Python's
"Galaxy Song" and Brian Cox with his wheelchair.[397][398]
