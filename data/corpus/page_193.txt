Hawking was also a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences (1984),[405] the American
Philosophical Society (1984),[406] and the U.S. National Academy of Sciences (1992).[407] He was
awarded Honorary membership in 1992, of the Manchester Literary and Philosophical Society.[408] In
the 1989 Birthday Honours, he was appointed a Member of the Order of the Companions of Honour
(CH).[160][409]
In 1981, Hawking was awarded the American Franklin Medal,[410]
and in the 1982 New Year Honours, appointed a Commander of the
Order of the British Empire (CBE).[275][411][412] He then received
five more honorary degrees,[158] the Gold Medal of the Royal
Astronomical Society (1985),[413] the Paul Dirac Medal (1987)[158]
and, jointly with Penrose, the prestigious Wolf Prize (1988).[414] In
1986, he was appointed an Academician at the Pontifical Academy
of Sciences.[415] He reportedly declined a knighthood in the late
1990s in objection to the UK's science funding policy.[416][417] In
2002, following a UK-wide vote, the BBC included Hawking in their
list of the 100 Greatest Britons.[418] He was awarded the Copley
Medal from the Royal Society (2006),[419] the Presidential Medal of
Freedom, which is America's highest civilian honour (2009),[420]
and the Russian Special Fundamental Physics Prize (2013).[421]
Several buildings have been named after him, including the
Stephen W. Hawking Science Museum in San Salvador,
El Salvador,[422] the Stephen Hawking Building in Cambridge,[423]
and the Stephen Hawking Centre at the Perimeter Institute in
Canada.[424] Given Hawking's association with time, he unveiled the mechanical "Chronophage" (or
time-eating) Corpus Clock at Corpus Christi College, Cambridge in September 2008.[425][426] In July
2017, Hawking was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Imperial College London.[427]
Hawking received the 2015 BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award in Basic Sciences shared
with Viatcheslav Mukhanov for discovering that the galaxies were formed from quantum fluctuations in
the early Universe. At the 2016 Pride of Britain Awards, Hawking received the lifetime achievement
award "for his contribution to science and British culture".[428] After receiving the award from Theresa
May, Hawking humorously requested that she not seek his help with Brexit.[428]
