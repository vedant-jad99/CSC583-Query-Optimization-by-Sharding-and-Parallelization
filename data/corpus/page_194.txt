In 2017, the Cambridge Union Society, in conjunction with Hawking, established the Professor Stephen
Hawking Fellowship. The fellowship is awarded annually to an individual who has made an exceptional
contribution to the STEM fields and social discourse,[429] with a particular focus on impacts affecting the
younger generations. Each fellow delivers a lecture on a topic of their choosing, known as the "Hawking
Lecture".[430]
Hawking himself accepted the inaugural fellowship, and he delivered the first Hawking Lecture in his
last public appearance before his death.[431][432]
Hawking was a member of the advisory board of the Starmus Festival, and had a major role in
acknowledging and promoting science communication. The Stephen Hawking Medal for Science
Communication is an annual award initiated in 2016 to honour members of the arts community for
contributions that help build awareness of science.[433] Recipients receive a medal bearing a portrait of
Hawking by Alexei Leonov, and the other side represents an image of Leonov himself performing the
first spacewalk along with an image of the "Red Special", the guitar of Brian May (with music being
another major component of the Starmus Festival).[434]
The Starmus III Festival in 2016 was a tribute to Stephen Hawking and the book of all Starmus III
lectures, "Beyond the Horizon", was also dedicated to him. The first recipients of the medals, which were
awarded at the festival, were chosen by Hawking himself: composer Hans Zimmer, physicist Jim Al-
Khalili, and the science documentary film Particle Fever (2013).[435]
